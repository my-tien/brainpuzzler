This is the Brainpuzzler project. Our aim is to get a better understanding of brain cell networks.

With the help of machine learning techniques, we have produced computational attempts on brain reconstruction — a very fast but error-prone approach.

This is where you step in: Humans have naturally strong pattern recognition capabilities. You can correct the computated results by putting puzzle pieces of it together.

For this purpose we provide KNOSSOS, a manual brain reconstruction software from the Max Planck Institute for medical research in Heidelberg, Germany.

Everyone can contribute to this project — without any background in neuroscience!

This project is developed by My-Tien Nguyen at Max Planck Institute for Medical Research in Heidelberg, Germany. It is for her Bachelor's Thesis in Medical Informatics at Mannheim University of Applied Sciences.
