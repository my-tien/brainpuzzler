Connectomics is the science of reconstructing entire networks of brain cells, called 'neurons'. One great difficulty lies in the sheer number of neurons within a single creature. For example, larval zebrafish already have a complex nervous system with around 100'000 neurons. Another challenge is the small scale we have to work with: Neurons are measured in micrometers.

Computers are able to process these vast amounts of information. They perform extremely fast neuron reconstructions after they learned how to recognize brain cells.

But teaching computers to recognize objects in brain images is complex, the results are error-prone.

Humans, on the other hand, work very slowly compared to computers, but are naturally superior when it comes to pattern recognition.

So we came up with a solution that combines the strengths of both computers and humans:
