SegEM Challenge

The SegEM Challenge is an open competition to find the most suitable machine learning algorithms that segments 3D EM datasets. The competition is held by the Max Planck Institute for Medical Research located in Heidelberg, Germany.

About the competition

SegEM is a 3D image segmentation challenge, focusing on the tough segmentation problem of automatic neurite reconstruction from volume electron microscopy data of brain tissue. Since state of the art segmentation approaches start to approach the statistical significance limits of test metrics for small, densely annotated ground truth volumes, the SegEM test volume is a 10x10x10 Âµm cube with high skeleton reconstruction coverage. Submissions are tested against these skeleton reconstructions and reported as the sum of total false splits and merges.

Datasets

  • Provided ground truth xxx , provided by K. L. Briggman
  • j0126 striatum songbird data set, J. Kornfeld
  • 6 densely annotated cubes of size bla voxels with 32-bit integer labels, provided as raw matrices with little endian order in a .zip container.
  • dense myelin annotations (no membrane annotations) for approaches that can additionally use sparsely annotated ground truth

Submission format

  • 32-bit integer raw file, in the same format as the ground truth cubes, compressed in a .zip file.
  • Each neurite/supervoxel with its own ID.
  • 0 for background (extracellular space or membranes).

Evaluation

A skeleton node on 0 is counted as a false split.
